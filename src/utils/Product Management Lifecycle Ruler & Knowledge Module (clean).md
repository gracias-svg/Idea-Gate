0–6: From idea to prioritized bet
Stage	Domain	Core PM lens	When it happens	Key activities & questions	Frameworks (when used)	Artifacts & owners	Roles (incl. agile)	Decision owner	Job done / exit criteria
0. Idea capture & intake	Problem	Opportunity sensing (why this, why now)	Continuous; whenever any stakeholder surfaces an idea (outside sprint cadence).	- Capture ideas from founders, Sales, CS, Support, Eng, data. - Ask: “Is this a problem, a feature, or noise?” - Tag by product area, segment, potential metric impact.	- Idea funnel / triage board: always‑on to collect and organize ideas.
​ - North Star metric lens: used at triage to ask if this could move NSM or key inputs.
​	- Idea log / opportunity backlog (PM/PO). - 3–5 line idea blurb with context (submitter).	- PM/PO: owns intake, tagging, initial triage. - All functions (Sales, CS, Support, Eng, founders): submit ideas. - Scrum team: not yet formally involved.	PM/PO	- Idea logged, de‑duplicated, and tagged. - Initial decision: discard, park, or promote to discovery.
1. Discovery & problem framing	Problem	Problem space (are we solving the right thing?)	After an idea is promoted for investigation and is worth 1–2+ weeks of structured discovery.	- Market scanning & trend analysis. - Four‑level competitive analysis (direct competitors, substitutes, alternatives, in‑house hacks). - Generative user research (interviews, shadowing, ticket mining). - Ask: “Who is this for? What job are they hiring us for?”	- PESTEL: early for big/regulated/fast‑changing markets.
​ - Porter’s Five Forces + competitive mapping: when evaluating new product lines or major bets.
​ - SWOT: after initial market/competitor work to summarize internal vs external factors.
​ - JTBD + Mom Test: throughout discovery interviews to capture jobs without solution bias.
​ - Opportunity Solution Tree: near end of discovery to map outcomes → opportunities → solution options.
​	- Discovery brief / opportunity canvas (PM). - Market & competitive snapshot (PM/Strategy). - Research notes & interview summaries (UXR/PM). - Initial problem statement draft (PM).	- PM/PO: orchestrates discovery, writes brief. - UXR/Designer: runs and synthesizes research. - Data analyst: quantifies problem (usage/churn/tickets). - Strategy/BizOps: supports market/competitive analysis (common in Indian mid/large product orgs). - Agile: PO may schedule discovery spikes; dev team provides feasibility input.	Head/Director of Product (for big bets), PM/PO (for smaller bets)	- Clear evidence of a real, meaningful problem for a defined segment. - Strategic intent written: target user, problem, desired outcome direction. - Agreement to continue investing (or to stop).
2. Problem definition & 1‑pager	Problem	Clarity & focus (sharpening the bet)	After discovery synthesis; before deep solutioning.	- Synthesize into one precise problem statement. - Define personas, core use cases, constraints, non‑goals. - Ask: “What happens if we do nothing?” “Which users are out of scope?”	- Problem framing template: immediately after discovery. - JTBD statements: convert raw quotes to structured “When… I want to… so I can…”.
​ - Assumption mapping (known/unknown, impact): right after drafting 1‑pager.	- Problem 1‑pager (PM/PO). - Updated opportunity canvas (PM). - Risks & assumptions list with owners (PM/Eng/Design).	- PM/PO: owns 1‑pager. - Eng Lead/Architect: adds technical assumptions. - Designer/UXR: adds behavioural assumptions. - Agile: PO uses 1‑pager as upstream input to epics; SM may facilitate a framing workshop.	Head/Director of Product (for stream), PM/PO for squad scope	- Single, unambiguous problem statement agreed. - In‑scope and out‑of‑scope documented. - Top assumptions prioritized for validation.
3. Concept & solution ideation	Solution	Solution space (what options exist?)	After problem definition is frozen; before committing to one direction.	- Run structured ideation sessions with PM, Design, Eng (and sometimes Sales/CS). - Sketch storyboards, flow variants, including manual/low‑tech options. - Ask: “What are 3–5 distinct credible approaches?”	- Design Thinking / Double Diamond: to diverge and converge after problem clarity.
​ - Crazy 8s, collaborative sketching: inside workshops. - Value Proposition Canvas: once a few concepts exist, to map pains/gains.	- Concept sketches and user flows (Design). - Solution concept doc with trade‑offs (PM/Design/Eng).	- Product Designer: leads ideation and flows. - PM/PO: ensures concepts match problem and constraints. - Eng Lead: validates feasibility early. - CS/Sales: bring front‑line perspective. - Agile: dev team participates as contributors; no sprint commitments yet.	PM/PO (within guardrails set by Head of Product)	- At least 2–3 solution directions evaluated. - Preferred concept (or combination) chosen with explicit rationale. - Key solution hypotheses documented.
4. MVP hypothesis & value definition	Solution	Impact & bets (what outcome we seek)	Immediately after choosing preferred concept; before heavy build or expensive experiments.	- Turn concept into 1–2 primary hypotheses tied to metrics. - Define MVP vs “nice‑to‑have” at outcome level. - Ask: “What must be true for this to be a win?”	- Hypothesis templates (“We believe… We’ll know… when…”): right after solution selection. - North Star & input metrics: while defining success criteria; keep aligned with business outcomes.
​ - MVP test cards: planning specific experiments / pilots.	- Hypothesis & success criteria sheet (PM/Data). - Early metric & instrumentation plan (PM/Data/Eng).	- PM/PO: defines hypotheses & metrics. - Data analyst: sanity‑checks measurability and timelines. - Eng Lead: confirms feasibility of instrumentation and experiments. - Agile: PO later maps hypotheses to experiment/user stories.	PM/PO (reviewed by Head of Product for large bets)	- MVP outcome and success metrics are agreed and documented. - Clear “double‑down / pivot / stop” rules tied to metric thresholds.
5. Research, validation & sizing	Problem + Solution	Risk reduction (do the numbers work?)	After hypotheses are defined; before committing full team capacity. Often iterative.	- Qual + quant validation (interviews, surveys, fake doors, concierge tests). - Demand tests (LPs, waitlists, pricing experiments). - Directional TAM/SAM/SOM and unit economics.	- TAM/SAM/SOM & simple business model: for strategic / large‑budget bets.
 - A/B & smoke tests, fake doors: when you can cheaply measure interest (clicks, signups). - WTP / pricing probes: where revenue risk is high.	- Validation summary & recommendation (PM/UXR). - Early business case / lean canvas (PM/Finance). - Experiment logs and metrics snapshots (PM/Data).	- PM/PO: orchestrates validation and writes recommendation. - UXR: designs and runs research. - Data: designs experiments and analyses. - Finance/Strategy: supports economics. - Eng: implements experimental surfaces. - Agile: discovery work may run as spikes in sprints; SM protects capacity.	Head/Director of Product (for big bets), PM/PO + squad for local bets	- Evidence threshold reached with clear go/kill/reshape recommendation. - Unit economics and market potential directionally sound for given investment. - Leadership agrees to put it on roadmap or to stop.
6. Prioritization & roadmap framing	Portfolio	Portfolio trade‑offs (what we do first)	In quarterly/PI planning and roadmap reviews; after validation is done.	- Compare this initiative against others in same timeframe. - Decide themes and release waves. - Ask: “What not to do now?”	- RICE: for cross‑initiative, cross‑team prioritization; good for Q/half‑year roadmap.
 - MoSCoW: to slice scope inside a given initiative/release (MVP vs later).
​ - Kano: earlier in discovery to identify basic vs delighter features feeding into RICE/MoSCoW.
​ - Impact vs Effort: quick visualization before detailed scoring.
​	- Prioritized initiative list with scores (PM). - Outcome‑based roadmap (PM). - Roadmap decision log (PM/Leadership).	- PM/PO: runs scoring and drafts roadmap. - Eng Lead: provides effort ranges and risks. - Design Lead: highlights UX complexity. - Product leadership (Head/VP): approves roadmap. - Agile: PO translates roadmap into epics; SM aligns cadence & capacity.	Head/VP of Product	- Initiative has a clear position on roadmap (now/next/later). - High‑level scope and time horizon agreed. - Key dependencies and sequencing documented.
7–13: From spec to ready‑to‑launch build
Stage	Domain	Core PM lens	When it happens	Key activities & questions	Frameworks (when used)	Artifacts & owners	Roles (incl. agile)	Decision owner	Job done / exit criteria
7. Detailed scope & PRD	Solution	Requirements clarity (what exactly we’ll ship)	After roadmap commitment; before deep design and build; refined continuously.	- Break initiative into epics and initial stories. - Clarify constraints, edge cases, non‑functional requirements. - Align understanding with Eng & Design.	- User story templates & INVEST: when decomposing epics into stories pre‑planning.
​ - Acceptance criteria templates: as stories near implementation.	- PRD / product brief (PM/PO). - Epics and high‑level stories (PM/PO). - Non‑functional requirements (PM/Eng).	- PM/PO: owns PRD and backlog. - Eng Lead/Architect: signs off on feasibility. - Designer: ensures UX implications captured. - Agile: PO is backlog owner; SM facilitates refinement sessions; dev team reviews and challenges.	PM/PO	- PRD/brief accepted by PM, Eng, Design. - Epics and key stories clear and testable. - Success metrics & constraints clearly documented.
8. UX flows, IA & low‑fi design	Solution	User experience (how it works)	After PRD is stable; before high‑fi; some overlap with early tech spikes.	- Map end‑to‑end journeys and states (happy/unhappy). - Define IA and navigation. - Create low‑fi wireframes.	- User journey maps & service blueprints: when PRD and user goals are clear. - Flow/state diagrams: for complex logic, permissions, multi‑step flows. - Nielsen heuristics: checklist before usability testing.	- UX flows & low‑fi wireframes (Designer). - IA diagrams (Designer).	- Designer/UX: leads flows and IA. - PM/PO: validates flows vs problem/PRD. - Eng Lead: checks feasibility and identifies edge cases. - Agile: dev team participates in reviews; SM ensures time in sprints.	PM/PO + Design Lead for UX decisions	- Primary and critical edge flows mapped and agreed. - Team agrees flows address problem and constraints. - Ready to move to high‑fi design and usability tests.
9. High‑fi design & usability validation	Solution	Usability & desirability	After low‑fi flows; before full implementation commitment; may overlap with backend build.	- Produce high‑fi UI using design system. - Build clickable prototypes. - Run usability tests; iterate on design.	- Design Sprint: when UX/solution risk is high and fast validation is needed.
​ - Usability test methods (SUS, task success): on clickable prototypes. - Design system & components: throughout design & build.	- High‑fi mockups & interactive prototypes (Designer). - Usability test plans & reports (Designer/UXR). - Design specs & tokens (Designer).	- Designer: owns visual design and prototypes. - UXR: plans and runs usability tests. - PM/PO: prioritizes what must be fixed pre‑build. - Eng: early feasibility review. - Agile: devs join reviews; SM coordinates design–dev syncs.	PM/PO + Design Lead	- High‑fi designs for in‑scope flows approved. - Critical usability issues resolved or consciously deferred. - Design assets are dev‑ready.
10. Technical discovery & architecture	Delivery	Feasibility & architecture	After PRD & key UX flows; before major implementation sprints; often across 1–2 sprints.	- Explore tech options, integrations, data, constraints. - Define system architecture, data models, APIs. - Estimate effort & risk; identify unknowns.	- Architecture review checklist: when finalizing architecture for the first release.
​ - Spikes: when technical unknowns need time‑boxed research. - Risk matrix: throughout to track tech risk.	- High‑level architecture doc (Eng Lead/Architect). - API contracts & data model drafts (Eng). - Estimate ranges & risk log (Eng/PM).	- Eng Lead/Architect: owns architecture and trade‑offs. - Senior engineers: run spikes. - PM/PO: aligns architecture with business needs. - Agile: dev team executes spikes in sprints; SM protects capacity.	Engineering Lead / Architect	- Architecture and major tech choices documented and agreed. - No “unknown‑unknowns” on critical path. - Estimates good enough for sprint/release planning.
11. Backlog creation & release planning	Delivery	Execution planning	After architecture; before major build; refined in PI/quarterly planning and grooming.	- Decompose epics into detailed stories with acceptance criteria and estimates. - Decide release slices (MVP, beta, GA) and milestones. - Map dependencies.	- User story mapping: after PRD/flows, before implementation, to build a walking skeleton and later slices.
​ - Capacity/velocity planning: before PI/quarterly planning and launches.	- Groomed product backlog (PM/PO). - Release plan & milestones (PM/PO). - Dependency list (PM/Eng).	- PM/PO: prioritizes backlog and sets release objectives. - Dev team: estimates and raises dependencies. - Eng Lead & Designer: ensure tech/UX dependencies visible. - Agile: PO owns backlog; SM facilitates planning; dev team commits.	PM/PO + Engineering Lead for commitment scope	- Stories prioritized and sized; acceptance criteria exist. - MVP/first release scope locked. - Team understands the release plan.
12. Implementation (build)	Delivery	Delivery & scope control	Once planning is done and sprints are running; repeats per sprint until release scope complete.	- Sprint planning, daily stand‑ups, reviews, retros. - Build code, tests, docs. - Manage trade‑offs on scope/time/quality.	- Scrum or Kanban: throughout build.
 - DoR & DoD: per story for start/finish gates. - T‑shirt sizing, burndown/burnup: for planning and tracking.	- Sprint boards & burndown/burnup (Scrum team). - Codebase & automated tests (Dev team). - Updated PRD/decision log (PM/PO).	- Dev team (Engineers, QA, embedded Designer): delivers increments. - PM/PO: clarifies scope, accepts stories. - Scrum Master: runs ceremonies, removes blockers.	Scrum team (collective commitment), PM/PO on acceptance	- All committed stories for release meet DoD. - Integrated build passes internal tests. - No critical technical risks remain for planned launch.
13. QA, UAT & hardening	Delivery	Quality & risk	After feature implementation; before go/no‑go; may be one or more “hardening” sprints.	- Run functional, integration, regression, performance/security tests. - Conduct UAT with internal/pilot customers. - Triage and fix bugs; decide which issues block launch.	- Test plans & traceability matrices: for structured testing.
​ - Bug triage matrix (severity/priority): during defect reviews.	- Test cases & execution reports (QA/SDET). - UAT scripts & sign‑off notes (PM/CS/Sales). - Known issues list with severities & mitigations (PM/Eng).	- QA/SDET: leads testing. - Dev team: fixes defects, improves automation. - PM/PO: prioritizes which issues must block launch. - CS/Sales/internal users: perform UAT. - Scrum Master: facilitates bug‑bash/triage.	PM/PO + Eng Lead + QA Lead	- Agreed test coverage achieved. - High‑severity bugs resolved or accepted with mitigation. - UAT sign‑off from relevant stakeholders.
14–18: GTM, launch, and iteration
Stage	Domain	Core PM lens	When it happens	Key activities & questions	Frameworks (when used)	Artifacts & owners	Roles (incl. agile)	Decision owner	Job done / exit criteria
Stage	Domain	Core PM lens	When it happens	Key activities & questions	Frameworks (when used)	Artifacts & owners	Roles (incl. agile)	Decision owner	Job done / exit criteria
14. Pre‑launch GTM planning	GTM	Market readiness	2–6 weeks before launch, overlapping with late QA/hardening.	- Define target segments, positioning, messaging. - Decide pricing & packaging for this release. - Plan channels, campaigns, enablement.	- 4 Ps (Product, Price, Place, Promotion): early in GTM planning.
​ - Positioning statement (e.g., Moore): when locking narrative. - Launch tier matrix (Tier 1/2/3): at GTM start to choose blast radius and investment.
​	- GTM brief & launch plan (PMM/PM). - Pricing & packaging sheet (PMM/Finance). - Sales battlecards & decks (PMM/Sales). - Support FAQs & help‑center content (Support/PMM).	- PMM: leads GTM. - PM/PO: ensures product truth and scope. - Sales & CS leadership: align on enablement. - Marketing: owns campaigns. - Agile: PO aligns milestones; SM coordinates where needed.	PMM + Head of Product (jointly)	- Audience, messaging, positioning, and tier locked. - Pricing/packaging agreed. - All GTM work items have owners and dates.
15. Launch readiness & go/no‑go	GTM + Delivery	Operational alignment	In days before launch; may have one or more reviews for big launches.	- Run cross‑functional readiness review (Eng, PM, PMM, Support, Sales, Legal, Ops). - Verify checklists for tech, support, GTM. - Review risk register and rollback/kill‑switch plan. - Take explicit go/no‑go decision.	- Launch readiness checklist: at final reviews. - Risk register & rollback plan: for medium/high‑risk launches.
​	- Go/no‑go deck & decision log (PM). - Runbook (rollout & rollback) (Eng/PM). - Monitoring & alert dashboards (Eng/Data).	- PM/PO: runs go/no‑go. - Eng Lead: certifies technical readiness. - PMM: certifies GTM readiness. - Support & Sales leads: certify readiness. - Scrum Master: facilitates meeting and follow‑ups.	Head/VP of Product (or GM), with PM/Eng/PMM sign‑off	- Explicit go/no‑go documented. - Runbook and rollback plan approved. - Monitoring & alerting in place.
16. Launch (beta / GA)	GTM + Delivery	Execution & monitoring	At planned launch time; may be beta/staged/GA.	- Execute rollout via feature flags, staged cohorts, or big‑bang (per tier). - Run campaigns and announcements. - Monitor system health and product metrics closely.	- Feature flags & staged rollout: at launch to control risk. - Funnel & A/B testing setup: configured pre‑launch, used immediately post‑launch.	- Live product/feature in production (Eng). - Launch/campaign logs (PM/PMM). - Incident log / on‑call notes (Eng/SRE).	- Engineering/SRE: execute rollout, handle incidents. - PM/PO: monitors product metrics and adoption. - PMM/Marketing: run campaigns. - Support: handles early tickets. - Agile: dev team on‑call; SM may coordinate incident response.	PM/PO + Eng Lead during rollout	- Rollout complete to intended audience. - No unresolved P0 incidents after agreed observation window. - Early adoption/engagement signals visible.
17. Early post‑launch measurement	Learning	Outcome validation	1–4 weeks after launch, once enough data and feedback exist.	- Monitor adoption, activation, usage, retention, revenue, support tickets. - Aggregate qualitative feedback from CS/Support/Sales and users. - Compare results to hypotheses and targets.	- AARRR: for funnel (acquisition → revenue).
​ - HEART: for UX outcomes and satisfaction.
​ - Cohort analysis: for retention over time. - NPS/CSAT/CES: for sentiment and perceived effort.	- KPI dashboards & reports (Data/PM). - Post‑launch readout deck (PM). - Feedback summary (CS/PM).	- PM/PO: leads analysis & narrative. - Data: runs analyses. - CS/Support: provide structured feedback. - PMM: looks at GTM performance. - Agile: squad reviews in sprint review/retro.	PM/PO	- Hypotheses measured vs reality with a clear narrative. - List of fixes, improvements, and experiments identified. - Decision: double‑down, adjust, or consider rollback/sunset.
18. Iteration & optimization loop	Learning + Delivery	Continuous improvement	After first readout; repeats while product/feature is strategic.	- Prioritize improvements and follow‑up experiments. - Remove friction and improve conversion/retention/revenue. - Address tech debt uncovered during launch and early use.	- A/B & multivariate tests: for UX, messaging, pricing optimizations.
​ - Hook model / habit loops: for repeat‑engagement products. - Impact vs Effort / WSJF: to rank iteration and debt items.	- Iteration backlog (PM/PO). - Experiment designs & results (PM/Data). - Tech debt register (Eng).	- PM/PO: curates iteration backlog and defines “good enough” thresholds. - Dev & Design: implement improvements and experiments. - Data: designs and evaluates experiments. - Scrum Master: ensures improvement work is built into cadence.	PM/PO + Head of Product (on “stop investing” decisions)	- At least one iteration loop shipped and measured. - Key metrics trending toward/within targets or trade‑off explicitly accepted. - Major learnings fed back into discovery/problem definition for next cycle.
